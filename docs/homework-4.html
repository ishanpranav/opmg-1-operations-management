A tech company, Innovatech, manufactures two types of electronic devices: smartphones and tablets. Each smartphone is sold for $500 and each tablet is sold for $350. Question 2 Part D

Suppose EcoTiles is offered the opportunity to purchase an additional 1 liter of water at $1.10. Should they accept the offer? Why or why not? If yes, what would be the new optimal number of each type of tile to produce?

First, since 80 liters of water are being consumed, water is a binding constraint, and there are potential benefits from increased access to water.

Note also that an increase of 1 liter of water is less than the allowable increase of 22 liters. Thus, an optimal solution that uses 81 liters of water does not violate any existing binding constraints on the existing solution for 80 liters of water. If we purchase 1 additional liter of water, we do not need to recompute the entire solution.

Based on the sensitivity analysis in Part A, we know that the shadow price of the water constraint (3x1+2x2+4x3≤803x_1+2x_2+4x_3\leq 803x1​+2x2​+4x3​≤80) is 1.25. Since we are able to purchase 1 liter of water for less than the shadow price ($1.10), the decision to purchase is profitable, so we accept the offer.
